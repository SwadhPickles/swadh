Welcome to Swadh Pickles

Avakaya Multiverse is a flavourfull space where every jar of pickle represents the rich culinary heritage of Andhra kitchens. At SwadhPickles, we prepare traditional pickles using pure sesame (til) oil, fresh ingredients, and time-tested homemade methods with no added preservatives. Pickles are more than just a side dish in Telugu households. A small spoon of pickle with hot rice and ghee can turn a simple meal into something unforgettable. Here is our carefully crafted pickle menu, each with its own taste, tradition, and story.

Avakaya Specialties

Ulli Avakai

A delicious twist where small Garlic are added to mango avakai. The Garlic absorb the spicy masala, giving the pickle a slightly sweet and sharp taste.

Kaya Avakai

A traditional variation where mango pieces are cut smaller, allowing the spices to penetrate deeply, creating an intense and rich flavor.

Pacha Avakai

A fresh and vibrant version of avakai, made with raw green mangoes and a lighter spice mix. Perfect for those who enjoy natural mango flavor with balanced heat.

The Avakai

The king of pickles. Fresh raw mango pieces are mixed with mustard powder, red chilli powder, salt, and sesame oil. This bold and spicy pickle is a classic favorite with rice and ghee.

Bellam Avakai

A unique Andhra specialty combining jaggery (Bellam) with mango pickle spices. It creates a beautiful balance of sweet, spicy, and tangy flavors.

Pesara Avakai

A special preparation where green gram (Pesara pappu) is mixed with mango pieces and spices, adding a nutty texture and rich taste.

Noopapu Avakai

This version includes lentils blended with the mango pickle mixture, creating a thicker masala that coats the mango pieces beautifully.

Menthi Kaya

A pickle flavored with fenugreek seeds (Menthi), known for their slightly bitter yet aromatic taste that enhances the overall flavor.

Traditional Pachadis & Pickles

Magayi

A sun-dried mango pickle where the mango pieces are dried before mixing with spices. This process gives deep, concentrated mango flavor.

Noone Magayi

A richer version of magayi prepared with extra sesame oil, making the pickle smoother and longer lasting.

Gongura Pachadi

Made from the famous sorrel leaves (Gongura) of Andhra Pradesh, this pickle is tangy, spicy, and extremely popular with rice.

Tomato Pachadi

A homemade favorite made with ripe tomatoes, red chillies, and spices. It offers a perfect balance of tangy and spicy flavors.

Chintakaya Pachadi

Prepared from tender tamarind (Chintakaya), this pachadi has a sharp tanginess that pairs beautifully with hot rice.

Pandu Mirchi

A spicy pickle made with red chillies, blended with spices and sesame oil for a fiery kick.

Nimma Kaya

A classic lemon pickle, slow-cured in salt and spices until the lemons soften and absorb rich flavors.

Dabba Kaya

A traditional citron pickle, known for its thick peel and strong citrus aroma.

Ushira Kaya

Made from gooseberries (Amla), this pickle is both flavorful and nutritious

Allam Pachadi

A famous ginger chutney, known for its spicy warmth and digestive benefits.

Vadiyalu

Gummadi Vadiyalu

Traditional sun-dried crisps made from fresh pumpkin (Gummadi) mixed with rice flour and spices. These vadiyalu are light, crunchy, and mildly flavorful, perfect when deep-fried and served with hot rice and dal.

Saggu Biyyam Vadiyalu

Classic sago (Sabudana) crisps prepared by soaking saggu biyyam and blending it with spices before sun-drying. When fried, they puff up beautifully into crispy, airy snacks that pair wonderfully with meals or can be enjoyed as a crunchy snack

Pellala Vadiyalu

A delicious twist where small Garlic are added to mango avakai. The Garlic absorb the spicy masala, giving the pickle a slightly sweet and sharp taste.
